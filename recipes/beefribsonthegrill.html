Beef Ribs on the Grill

Description

We will be beginning a journey that ends with a flavorful, smokey, tasty, juicy plate of beef ribs that were grilled right in your backyard.

Other than that FIRST BITE -

What could be better than a nice drink, with a nice seat, while waiting for a nice slab of beef ribs to finish their cook?

Tell me I'm wrong - You can't, and you know it.

Ingredients

  • 2 racks beef back ribs (about 7 lbs. total)
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of your favorite bbq rub - just to ensure that they have plenty of flavor
  • 1/2 cup of bbq sauce (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon of olive oil

Steps

  1. Rinse the ribs
  2. Pat dry with paper towels
  3. Apply olive oil to the ribs
  4. Apply all of the seasoning
  5. Go fire up the the grill while the seasonings soak into the meat. Use enough charcoal neccessary for a hot cook (400-450 degrees).
  6. Once the grill is up to temp, cook both sides for 5 minutes each to seal the juices in. The close the vents on the grill about 25-30% to drop the temp to 300-325 and them cook for another 45 minutes.

Apply more charcoal if the temps dip below low heat range.

Once the ribs are done, let them rest for 10 minutes.

Now, take a bite - and another. Try not to bite a finger.
